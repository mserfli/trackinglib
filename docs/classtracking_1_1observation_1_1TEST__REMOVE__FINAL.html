class tracking::observation::TEST_REMOVE_FINAL< CovarianceMatrixPolicy_, StateDef_ >

Observation model for a polar bearing-only measurement.

Observation model for a direct velocity measurement (vx, vy)

Observation model for a polar range-only measurement.

Nonlinear observation model measuring the bearing of the position: h(x) = atan2(y, x)

The bearing innovation is wrapped into [-pi, pi] by the shadowed computeInnovation().

Nonlinear observation model measuring the polar coordinates of the position and the radial (doppler) velocity: h(x) = [sqrt(x^2 + y^2), atan2(y, x), (x*vx + y*vy)/sqrt(x^2 + y^2)]'

The bearing innovation is wrapped into [-pi, pi] by the shadowed computeInnovation(). If the StateDef provides no velocity, the doppler component is predicted as zero with a zero Jacobian row: the zero row yields a zero gain column, so the doppler measurement contributes nothing to the state update (it is effectively ignored, it does NOT contribute via its variance). With a correlated R the decorrelation may still mix the doppler component into the range/bearing rows - that is correct conditioning on the correlated component, not a defect.
